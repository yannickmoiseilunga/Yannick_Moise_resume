About me

I am currently pursuing a PhD in Chemical Engineering specializing in Sustainable Process Engineering at the University of the Witwatersrand. My PhD research involves developing an automated solution strategy for the optimization of industrial water networks.
I completed my Master and Bachelor of Science degree in Chemical Engineering at the University of Cape Town specializing in Mathematical Programming applied to process design. Current research in my PhD field involves optimizing large and detailed models which require similar techniques as the ones used in deep learning to find good initial feasible points. This led me to nurture an interest in the field of algorithm development, data science and Artificial Intelligence. I have gained exposure to machine learning in Python using TensorFlow for object detection & classification through competitions on platforms such as Kaggle and Mediaeval. It is my hope to solve real-world business problems and develop smarter engineering software tools and intelligent systems.
I have a genuine passion for problem solving as well as mathematical modelling and computer simulations. My interest in the development of mathematical models started during my undergraduates when I was exposed to coding nonlinear programming problems in Matlab. I was fascinated with the process of programming a series of operations that could be initiated at the click of a button. As a result, I knew what I wanted to do for a living.
